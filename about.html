About the Hospital

A legacy of over 70 years of free, accessible healthcare for the people of Erode.

Thanthai Periyar Government Headquarters Hospital

Originally functioning near the Abdul Gani Textile Market in Panneerselvam Park, the hospital was shifted to its present location on EVN Road, Kaikolar Thottam in 1955. Named after the great social reformer Thanthai Periyar E.V. Ramasamy — who himself sponsored a share for this hospital — it stands as a symbol of the Dravidian movement's commitment to social equity and public welfare.

Today, it functions as the District Headquarters Hospital of Erode and is classified as a 700-bed Tertiary Care Hospital with 75 medical officers. It serves not only Erode district but also patients from Tiruppur, Namakkal, Salem, and Coimbatore who seek free specialist care.

In 2019, the Government of Tamil Nadu announced its upgrade to a Multi Super Speciality Hospital. A B+G+8 storeyed additional block of 2,32,602 sq ft was constructed at a cost of ₹80.81 Crores, and was inaugurated by Chief Minister M.K. Stalin in 2024, bringing cardiac surgery, neurosurgery, and other super-speciality services directly to government patients.

During the COVID-19 pandemic (April 2020), the hospital was designated as a Corona Speciality Hospital with 184 dedicated isolation beds, playing a pivotal role in Erode's pandemic response alongside Erode Medical College Hospital.

The hospital operates under the oversight of the Tamil Nadu Directorate of Medical and Rural Health Services and adheres to all national and state health protocols for patient care, infrastructure, and biomedical waste management.
