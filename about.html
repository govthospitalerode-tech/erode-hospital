About the Hospital

A legacy of over 150 years of healthcare — now Tamil Nadu's largest secondary care hospital.

Thanthai Periyar Government Headquarters Hospital

Erode General Hospital was founded in 1872 by the East India Company, making it one of the oldest public hospitals in Tamil Nadu. In August 1928, the hospital was taken over by the government from the British East India Company. Originally functioning near the Abdul Gani Textile Market in Panneerselvam Park, it was shifted to its present location on EVN Road, Kaikolar Thottam in 1955.

The foundation stone for the present building was laid on 9th July 1955 by then Chief Minister Mr. K. Kamaraj, and the hospital was formally inaugurated on 15th February 1960 by then Health Minister Mr. Manikkavelu. Named after the great social reformer Thanthai Periyar E.V. Ramasamy — who himself sponsored a share for this hospital — it stands as a symbol of the Dravidian movement's commitment to social equity and public welfare.

In 1973, when the Government Headquarters Hospital Coimbatore was converted into a medical college hospital, Erode hospital became the District Headquarters Hospital for Coimbatore district. With the bifurcation of Coimbatore on 17th September 1979, Periyar district was formed and the hospital became the Erode District Headquarters Hospital.

Situated in the heart of Erode city, spread over 6.5 acres, the hospital is accessible from both the railway station and bus stand. Key specialised services added over the years include: a Haemodialysis Unit (since 2007, now with 21 machines), Upper GI Endoscopy (since 2007), ECHO Cardiogram (since June 2012), NCD Screening (since June 2012), and the CMCHIS cashless scheme (since July 2012).

During the COVID-19 pandemic (April 2020), the hospital was designated as a Corona Speciality Hospital with 184 dedicated isolation beds, serving all of Erode district alongside Erode Medical College Hospital.

In March 2024, a B+G+8 storeyed Super Speciality block of 2,32,602 sq ft was inaugurated by Chief Minister M.K. Stalin at a cost of ₹80.81 Crores. With this, the hospital now has 1,085 total beds (960 functional) and is the largest secondary care hospital in Tamil Nadu by bed strength. Super-speciality services now include Cardiology, Neurology, Medical Oncology, Neurosurgery, Plastic Surgery, Urology, and Paediatric Surgery. A separate 20-bed Pay Ward with air-conditioned attached rooms, television, and geysers was established in September 2024.

Key Milestones

1872
Founded by the East India CompanyErode General Hospital established — one of the oldest public hospitals in Tamil Nadu.
1928
Taken Over by GovernmentIn August 1928, the hospital was transferred from the British East India Company to the Government of India.
1955
Shifted to Present Location · Foundation Stone LaidMoved to EVN Road, Kaikolar Thottam. Foundation stone laid on 9 July 1955 by CM K. Kamaraj. Periyar E.V. Ramasamy sponsored a share for the hospital. Started as a 220-bed Taluk Government Hospital.
1960
Formally InauguratedInaugurated on 15 February 1960 by Health Minister Mr. Manikkavelu.
1979
Erode District Headquarters HospitalWith bifurcation of Coimbatore on 17 September 1979, Periyar district was created and the hospital became the Erode District Headquarters Hospital.
2007
Haemodialysis & Endoscopy Units LaunchedHaemodialysis unit with 3 machines started (now 21 machines). Upper GI Endoscopy unit also launched, providing diagnostic and therapeutic services for chronic kidney disease patients.
2012
CMCHIS · ECHO · NCD ProgrammeCM's Comprehensive Health Insurance Scheme (CMCHIS) launched (July). ECHO Cardiogram unit started (June). NCD screening programme for diabetes, hypertension, breast cancer, and cervical cancer initiated.
2020
COVID-19 Speciality HospitalDesignated as Corona Speciality Hospital with 184 isolation beds. Pivotal role in Erode district's pandemic response.
2022
Zero Maternal Mortality · Bed ExpansionAchieved zero maternal deaths for two consecutive quarters — recognised by TN Directorate of Health Services. Bed strength increased to 725.
2024
Super Speciality Block Inaugurated · 1,085 BedsB+G+8 storeyed super speciality block (2,32,602 sq ft) inaugurated by CM M.K. Stalin at ₹80.81 Crores. Hospital now has 1,085 beds — the largest secondary care hospital in Tamil Nadu. 20-bed Pay Ward opened in September 2024.
